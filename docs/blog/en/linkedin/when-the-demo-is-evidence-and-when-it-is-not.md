# LinkedIn Pack - When the Demo Is Evidence and When It Is Not

## Broad Post

Demos are useful because they make product ideas testable.

They become dangerous when they quietly become policy.

A prototype can reveal usability problems, workflow friction, or a better interaction. That is valuable evidence.

But if the spec and docs are never updated, the team slowly starts governing the product through whatever screen happens to exist.

## Institutional Post

When reviewing a prototype, one crucial question is:

Are we looking at an approved workflow model, or just the current implementation state?

That distinction matters for governance, procurement confidence, and long-term maintenance.

## Student Post

One of the most important lessons in software practice is that a working prototype is not self-justifying.

It might be right.
It might be temporary.
It might be incomplete.

The important step is turning a prototype insight into an explicit documented decision.

## Thread Outline

1. Visible artifacts are persuasive.
2. That is why demos can become accidental authority.
3. A demo is great for usability evidence.
4. It is not enough to define product truth on its own.
5. Better workflow discovered in a demo should update the spec.
