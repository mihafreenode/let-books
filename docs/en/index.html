Why Let Books exists

Too many valuable educational books end up forgotten in basements, cabinets, garages, offices, or family collections. Let Books is designed to make the practical side of preservation easier: inventory, review, retrieval, and handoff.

Books as real objects

Physical copies matter. One edition can have many real copies, each with a different condition, box, and donation outcome.

QR-based storage workflow

Scan a box, see what is inside, add another book quickly, and later retrieve requested books without guessing where they went.

Library-friendly review

Export structured lists, collect wanted or maybe decisions, import them back, and generate pick lists grouped by location.

Who it is for

Let Books is designed for people who want to donate books, institutions that review and receive them, and administrators who need governance and operational clarity.

Individuals

Private donors, families, volunteers, and small organizations cataloging books and preparing them for review.

Institutions

Libraries first, plus schools, universities, archives, and NGOs reviewing offers and coordinating retrieval.

Administrators

Tenant owners, host organizations, and future operators managing access, privacy, and delivery workflows.

How the workflow works

The first successful workflow is practical and storage-aware. It works even before deep integrations or advanced AI are added.

1

Scan a box QR code

Open the box page immediately and confirm which location you are working in.

2

Add books on phone

Take a cover photo, scan or enter ISBN when available, and save quickly with minimal typing.

3

Track exact storage

Keep room, shelf, box, and sequence information tied to each physical copy.

4

Export for review

Create a donation batch and export a spreadsheet for a library or partner institution.

5

Import decisions

Bring back wanted, maybe, and not wanted decisions while preserving stable item identity.

6

Create a pick list

Group requested books by storage location so retrieval and handoff become manageable.

Useful even without AI

  • Manual book entry
  • Photo upload
  • Storage tracking
  • Excel export and import
  • Library decision tracking
  • Pick lists and delivery status

Where AI helps later

  • OCR from title pages and covers
  • Metadata suggestions from external sources
  • Translation suggestions for selected fields
  • Condition suggestions that still require human review

Core principles

Privacy by default

Exact storage details, donor home information, and private notes should not become public by accident.

Provenance matters

Libraries and archives care whether metadata came from a human, OCR, import, or external catalog.

Mobile-first practicality

The main work often happens standing near shelves and boxes. The phone workflow is not secondary.

Example: A family inherits a professor's technical library. They label each box, add books room by room, export a batch for review, and later retrieve the requested books without unpacking everything twice.
