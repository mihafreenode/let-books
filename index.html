Why Let Books Exists

Educational books often remain useful long after they stop being actively used. Let Books is focused on the practical work needed to preserve them, reduce waste, make donation manageable, help libraries and communities review them, and keep real storage logistics visible from the beginning.

Preserve useful collections

Serious study books, textbooks, academic references, and multilingual collections should not disappear just because they are hard to sort.

Make donation practical

The goal is not only description. It is also being able to scan a box, find a copy, export a review list, and retrieve the right books later.

Support communities and libraries

Structured exports, review workflows, and storage-aware pick lists help books move from forgotten shelves to new readers.

Workflow Preview

The core workflow is intentionally practical, mobile-first, and useful even before production infrastructure or deeper integrations exist.

Scan a box QR code

Open the right box immediately and keep intake tied to a real location.

Add books from phone

Capture covers, scan ISBN where possible, and save quickly with minimal typing.

Export for review

Prepare structured spreadsheets for libraries and other reviewers without requiring accounts first.

Import review decisions

Bring wanted, maybe, and not-wanted decisions back into one consistent workflow.

Generate pick lists

Group requested books by room, shelf, and box so retrieval is realistic.

Retrieve requested books

Find the correct physical copy again instead of reopening every box from memory.
